HARRY POTTER

DESCRIPTION 

 Harry Potter is a globally famous fantasy series by J.K. Rowling, following orphan Harry Potter as he
discovers he's a wizard, attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, and battles Lord Voldemort, the
dark wizard who killed his parents. The seven books chronicle his coming-of-age, friendship with Ron Weasley
and Hermione Granger, and ultimate confrontation with evil, spawning blockbuster films, spin-offs, and a new
TV series, becoming the best-selling book series ever.
Key Aspects:
The Character: Harry Potter, "The Boy Who Lived," known for his lightning-bolt scar, is an orphan who learns
he's a wizard on his 11th birthday.
The World: A magical world hidden within the mundane, with magical creatures, spells, and institutions like
Hogwarts and the Ministry of Magic.
The Friends: His loyal companions, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, are central to his adventures.
The Conflict: The overarching story is Harry's destined battle against the evil Lord Voldemort, who seeks
immortality and power over Muggles (non-magical people).
The Books: A seven-book series, starting with Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (1997) and ending
with Deathly Hallows (2007).
The Films: Eight blockbuster movies (the last book split into two) released between 2001 and 2011,
maintaining a commitment to a predominantly British and Irish cast.
The Franchise: Expanded with spin-off films (like Fantastic Beasts), stage plays (The Cursed Child), theme
parks, and a new TV series in development for HBO.
Core Themes:
Love and sacrifice (Lily Potter's protection of Harry).
Friendship and loyalty.
Courage and destiny (Harry as the "Chosen One").
The battle between good and evil.
